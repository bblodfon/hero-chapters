Intro

Ideas I had since I don’t remember when, around the notion of heroes. I kept all my notes in different school books and papers here and there and at some point I took the effort to write them all in a text file and save them online. Years after that (and when the ideas were pretty much depleted because well, life happened) I decided to start this project, because you know, it’s better than leaving the project unfinished!

As I grew older, I found that the most important and captivating part of the stories that I was reading in books, seeing in TV series/movies, etc. was the character development. As such, I wanted to write a story that was mostly about exploring the characters themselves and how their powers (since they are heroes they have some) affect their everyday lives and the people around them. Do they use their powers responsibly? Does this mean for serving good purposes or maybe also bad? Who decides what is good or bad in such a hero-society?

The narrative is mostly given by the Buddha hero who encounters many of the other heroes during his adventures or searches or whatever! I never had a central plot to the whole book, more like chapters/pictures/glimpses of the situations and events these heroes fall into and usually it was the thought of a particular power that gave rise to a hero, his/her personality and at the end the event itself that he was the main protagonist. And as far as I remember, I wanted every chapter to have a different protagonist, a different hero that we would explore.

Let’s see how far imagination can take us!
— John
